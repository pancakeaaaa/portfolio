The Publishing Gateway: FANFICS TO BOOKS

My first ever publishing projects: From the trenches of Fan Fiction Websites to a literal book. Like many others in my generation, the beginning of my creative writing journey began with writing fan fiction on Archive of Our Own (Hereby, abbreviated to Ao3). Most fan fiction is published on websites such as Ao3 with just straight text in mind. CONSENT IS KEY!!!! Following similar parameters of the Zine Code of Ethics (CHICAGO CITATION), I made sure to contact each owner through social media to obtain consent (these are strictly personal projects AKA non-distributive).

Kalon by Ryerim

This 200,000 word fan fiction was my scripture during my senior year of high school. I wanted to memorialise it, and therefore, created the formatting of this book. Since it is a fan fiction, void of formalities of having a blurb, I combined the song I most associated with it, First Love Late Spring by Mitski (angsty, I know. I was a teenager haha). Kalon explores themes of co-dependency, mental illness and intimate relationships. Based on her own experiences with mental health and intimacy, Kalon contains an implicit dialectic nature, similar to a piece of ‚Äòformal literature‚Äô. As a teenager struggling weathering the IB programs and the shockwaves of the COVID epidemic, Kalon lent me a foundation to reflect on my own personal relationships and the ways in which I conducted them.

OTHER ZINES

1. Consent n Cumming of Age (CNC)

My first zine was a final for a Gender and Sexuality class. Consent and Cumming of Age addresses the lack of sexual education at the Jesuit University I was studying in at the time. Among the three big private universities in LA, ours was the only without any sexual education/resources or title 9 support. In response to this, we created a zine about consent and gave it out during the flea market Wednesdays on campus. And to supplement the lack of sexual education and support in the school, we taped a condom to the back of the zine ;)
